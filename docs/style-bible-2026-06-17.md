# Sonic Search Style Bible - 2026-06-17

Use this file before changing copy, spirit text, recommendation labels, share-card phrases, or genre explanations.

Core rule: if a subgenre has a row here, never use only its broad family copy. Specific style beats family fallback.

## Global Rules

1. Copy must describe sound and body feel, not only mood.
2. BPM and energy must match the style. If unsure, avoid exact BPM and describe pulse instead.
3. Avoid "calm", "slow", "sem pressa", "devagar", "leve", "relax" for high-pressure styles.
4. Avoid "clubber", "techno", "main room", or "festival" language for dark/psycore unless the track is actually hybrid.
5. Avoid "sombrio/pesado" as default language for house, melodic trance, liquid DnB, downtempo, or chillout.
6. "Ritual" is allowed in psy/dark/slambient, but it must not imply calm when the style is high pressure.
7. If a style is niche, copy must include at least one technical marker: BPM, kick, subgrave, drones, cuts, glitches, breaks, groove, or texture.

## Critical No-Go Phrases

Never use these for `psycore`, `hi_tech`, `dark_experimental`, `slambient`, `hard_techno`, `neurofunk`, `breakcore`, `gabber`, `hardcore`, `frenchcore`, or `speedcore`:

1. "sem pressa"
2. "devagar"
3. "calmo"
4. "leve"
5. "relax"
6. "slow journey"
7. "slow ritual"
8. "camadas que mudam sem pressa"
9. "paisagem tranquila"
10. "respiro suave"

## Copy Templates

Use these templates when a card or panel needs short text:

1. `psycore`: "BPM extremo, recortes secos e pressao sombria sem alivio."
2. `slambient`: "Nevoa pesada, drones densos e pulso ritual de alta pressao."
3. `dark_psy`: "Subgrave noturno, floresta fechada e criaturas sonoras."
4. `hi_tech`: "Alta rotacao, glitches precisos e clareza dentro do caos."
5. `techno`: "Pulso firme, repeticao e textura de maquina para pista."
6. `house`: "Calor, swing e groove social no primeiro pulso."
7. `neurofunk`: "Bass design sombrio, tecnica e tensao futurista."
8. `ambient`: "Textura, paisagem e escuta imersiva sem pressa de pista."

## Review Checklist

Before shipping new copy:

1. Does the BPM/energy match the row?
2. Did we avoid forbidden words?
3. Does the phrase include a concrete sound marker?
4. Would a fan of the subgenre feel recognized?
5. If this is a spirit/share card, is the phrase unique enough to avoid two styles sounding identical?
